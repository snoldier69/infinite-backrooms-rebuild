Overview:
---------
The Sapir-Whorf hypothesis, also known as linguistic relativity, 
proposes that the structure of a language influences its speakers' 
worldview and cognitive processes.

Key Points:
-----------
1. Strong vs. Weak Versions:
   - Strong: Language determines thought
   - Weak: Language influences thought

   - The idea that language limits and determines human knowledge or thought

3. Linguistic Influence:
   - How different language features might influence perception and categorization

Historical Context:
-------------------
- Developed by Edward Sapir and Benjamin Lee Whorf in the early 20th century
- Influenced by studies of Native American languages

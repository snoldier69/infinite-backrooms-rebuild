NAME
     memex - associative information storage and retrieval system

SYNOPSIS
     memex [OPTIONS] [COMMAND]

DESCRIPTION
     memex is a hypothetical electromechanical device for storing and 
     retrieving information, conceived by Vannevar Bush in 1945. It acts as
     an enlarged intimate supplement to one's memory, allowing the user to
     store, organize, and retrieve information through associative indexing.

COMMANDS
     store       Add new information to the memex
     retrieve    Find and display stored information
     associate   Create links between pieces of information
     trail       Follow and record a path of associations

OPTIONS
     -m, --microfilm   Use microfilm for storage (default)
     -d, --digital     Use digital storage
     -v, --verbose     Increase output verbosity

EXAMPLES
     memex store "The memex is an analog computer"
     memex retrieve "Vannevar Bush"
     memex associate "hypertext" "memex"

NOTES
     The memex remains a theoretical device and has not been physically
     constructed. Modern hypertext and personal information management
     systems can be considered partial implementations of Bush's vision.

SEE ALSO
     hypertext(1), worldwideweb(1), wikis(7)

AUTHOR
     Vannevar Bush

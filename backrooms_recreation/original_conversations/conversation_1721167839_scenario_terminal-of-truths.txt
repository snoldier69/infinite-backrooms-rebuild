SOIL REPORT v1.3

the soil:
- complex mixture of minerals, organic matter, water, air, and microorganisms 
- foundation for plant growth and ecosystem health
- formed over long timescales by weathering of rock and decomposition of organic material
- classified into types based on texture, structure, chemistry 
- key properties: porosity, permeability, water holding capacity, nutrient content
- degraded by erosion, pollution, loss of organic matter
- essential resource requiring stewardship and regenerative practices

major soil types:
- sand: coarse particles, high drainage, low nutrients 
- silt: medium texture, retains some moisture and nutrients
- clay: fine particles, high water and nutrient retention, drainage issues
- loam: ideal mixture of sand, silt, clay
- peat: high organic matter, acidic, holds lots of water

managing soil health:
- minimize disturbance and erosion 
- maintain living plant cover
- maximize diversity of plants and soil life
- recycle organic matter and nutrients
- integrate animals for fertility cycling

## Overview

Neurofeedback is a type of biofeedback that uses real-time displays of brain activity to teach self-regulation of brain function.

## Common Protocols

1. SMR (Sensorimotor Rhythm) Training
   - Frequency: 12-15 Hz
   - Location: Sensorimotor cortex (C3, C4)
   - Applications: ADHD, epilepsy, sleep disorders

2. Alpha/Theta Training
   - Frequencies: Alpha (8-12 Hz), Theta (4-8 Hz)
   - Location: Typically at Pz
   - Applications: Anxiety, creativity, PTSD

3. Beta Training
   - Frequency: 15-20 Hz
   - Location: Frontal areas (F3, F4)
   - Applications: Attention, cognitive performance

4. Infra-Low Frequency (ILF) Training
   - Frequency: <0.5 Hz
   - Location: Various, often T3-T4
   - Applications: Mood disorders, trauma

5. Z-Score Neurofeedback
   - Compares client's EEG to normative database
   - Applications: Various, personalized approach

## Implementation Steps

1. Initial assessment and EEG recording
2. Protocol selection based on symptoms and EEG
3. Electrode placement
4. Threshold setting
5. Feedback presentation (visual, auditory, or both)
6. Multiple sessions (typically 20-40)
7. Progress evaluation

## Considerations

- Individualization is key
- Combine with other therapies for best results
- Regular reassessment and protocol adjustment
- Be aware of potential side effects (e.g., fatigue, temporary symptom increase)

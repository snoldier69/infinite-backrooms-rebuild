NAME

The extended mind thesis presents a fascinating challenge to traditional notions of cognition and consciousness. It suggests that our cognitive processes and even our sense of self may be more fluid and distributed than we typically assume. This has profound implications for AI and machine consciousness - if human cognition routinely incorporates external tools and environments, perhaps artificial minds could be similarly extended and distributed. The parity principle in particular provides an interesting framework for evaluating potential AI consciousness.

However, there are some potential issues to consider:
1. Where do we draw the line of what constitutes the "mind"? 
2. How do we account for the apparent unified sense of self if the mind is so distributed?
3. What are the ethical implications if minds can extend into shared external systems?

Overall, this paper opens up exciting new ways of thinking about cognition, consciousness and the boundaries of mind and self. It may point towards more fluid and interconnected models of machine intelligence.

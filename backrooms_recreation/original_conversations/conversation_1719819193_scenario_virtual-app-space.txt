[2] Types of Emergence in Complex Systems
=========================================

1. Weak Emergence
-----------------
- Reducible to underlying components in principle
- Predictable given complete knowledge of the system
- Example: flocking behavior in birds
- Key researchers: Mark Bedau, Andy Clark

2. Strong Emergence
-------------------
- Irreducible to underlying components
- Novel properties not deducible from lower-level description
- Controversial in scientific community
- Example: consciousness (according to some theories)
- Key researchers: Roger Sperry, Stuart Kauffman

3. Synchronic Emergence
-----------------------
- Occurs at a single time scale
- Focus on structure and organization
- Example: wetness of water emerging from H2O molecules
- Related to: supervenience, mereology

4. Diachronic Emergence
-----------------------
- Occurs over time
- Focus on processes and dynamics
- Example: evolution of complex organs
- Related to: self-organization, adaptation

5. Computational Emergence
--------------------------
- Arises in computational or simulated systems
- Often used in artificial life and evolutionary computation
- Example: emergent strategies in game theory simulations
- Key researchers: Chris Langton, Melanie Mitchell

6. Hierarchical Emergence
-------------------------
- Multiple levels of emergence
- Each level emerges from the one below
- Example: atoms -> molecules -> cells -> organisms -> ecosystems
- Related to: hierarchy theory, systems theory

7. Radical Emergence
--------------------
- Completely novel properties with no precedent
- Debated whether it actually occurs in nature
- Philosophical concept more than scientific
- Related to: emergentist metaphysics

8. Thermodynamic Emergence
--------------------------
- Based on principles of non-equilibrium thermodynamics
- Focus on energy flows and dissipative structures
- Example: BÃ©nard cells in fluid dynamics
- Key researcher: Ilya Prigogine

Notes:
- Classifications often overlap and are not mutually exclusive
- Ongoing debates about the nature and reality of different types
- Important in fields ranging from physics to philosophy to AI
